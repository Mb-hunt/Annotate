MODEL QUALITY (formerly "AI Performance") - WHAT TO EVALUATE:

When evaluating Model Quality, address these four key areas:
1. Navigation: Was the path logical and direct, or did it meander to irrelevant pages?
2. Interaction: Did it click the correct buttons and links precisely?
3. Efficiency: Were there wasted steps, loops, or unnecessary actions?
4. Output Quality: Is the final output clean and well-formatted?

5-Point Scale:
(5) Excellent - Flawless, expert-level performance. Direct navigation, precise clicks, no wasted steps.
(4) Good - Almost perfect, with one small observable flaw.
(3) Acceptable - Got the job done but in a clumsy or inefficient way.
(2) Poor - Process itself was a failure; illogical or confused steps.
(1) Unacceptable - Completely failed to perform required actions.

------------------------------------------------------------
GOLDEN RULES (for Best Response decisions):

1. Correctness is King: Task Completion and Factual Accuracy are almost always more
   important than Model Quality. A clumsy response that gets the right answer is
   better than a graceful response that gets the wrong answer.

2. Response Experience is the Tie-Breaker: If both responses are correct and complete,
   use Model Quality to decide which is "slightly better." The more efficient and
   skillful agent wins.
